The city of AlexandriaThis was once the jewel of the Mediterranean. It is home to the Great Library of Alexandria and the huge Pharos Lighthouse – one of the seven wonders of the ancient world. More recently, from the late nineteenth century until the fifties of the twentieth century, Alexandria was the seat of an impressive group of writers, poets and artists who made the city their home.

Sharm El Sheikh is the main tourist center of the Sinai Peninsula and one of the best diving destinations in the world. You can see the underwater wonders of the Red Sea – particularly the waters of Ras Mohammed Marine Park to the south of the city – that have positioned Sharm as one of Egypt's top tourist destinations, and the diving and snorkeling found here still attracts swarms of diving enthusiasts every year.

Aswan City Spread along the banks of the Nile, Aswan is a cozy and friendly city that offers a tranquil space if you have just arrived from Luxor or busy Cairo. Once ancient Egypt enters Africa, the city is an ideal base for exploring the temples and monuments of the southern regions of Upper Egypt and the various Nubian culture in the region.
